## 1. Workflow Audit

Twelve recurring tasks from my real week (study, internship, side projects), each classified with a one-line rationale.

| # | Task | Classification | Why (one-line rationale) |
|---|------|----------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Preparing for quizzes | Collaborate | AI makes practice questions and explains my mistakes — but the understanding has to end up in my head. |
| 2 | Learning (courses) | Collaborate | AI explains confusing parts and summarizes — but watching, thinking, and connecting ideas is mine. |
| 3 | University assignments | Collaborate | AI helps me understand and checks my work — but the work must be mine, for integrity and because I'm the one examined. |

| 9 | Messages and emails | Delegate with review | AI can draft formal emails, I review and send; personal chats stay fully mine. |
| 10 | Organizing files / disk space | Fully automate | Scanning and cleanup rules can run on their own, with approval only for deletions. |
| 11 | Searching opportunities | Delegate with review | AI can search and shortlist far faster; only I can judge what actually fits my goals. |
| 12 | Planning my week | Just me | My goals, my energy, my deadlines — AI can format a schedule, but the choosing is mine. |

## 2. Three Target Tasks (reused in FL-02 → FL-04)

**Target 1 — Internship assignments** *(Collaborate)*
Done well = I complete the assigned task within its estimated hours, I make every judgment call myself (lane / approach), and I can point to at least one thing I verified or corrected in the AI's output before submitting. Zero unreviewed AI output is submitted.

**Target 2 — Messages and emails** *(Delegate with review)*
Done well = AI drafts it, I edit for accuracy and tone, and every fact is checked before sending. Success = a formal email drafted and reviewed in under 5 minutes, with zero factual errors, that still sounds like me.

**Target 3 — Searching opportunities** *(Delegate with review)*
Done well = AI returns a shortlist, I filter it against my actual goals, and I finish with 3–5 genuinely-fitting opportunities I can act on — each one justified in a single sentence for why it fits.
